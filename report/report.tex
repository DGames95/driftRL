\section{Vehicle model}
The simulation uses a 3-DOF single-track (bicycle) model with body-frame
states $(v_x, v_y, r)$ and global pose $(x, y, \psi)$:
\begin{align}
\dot{v}_x &= \tfrac{1}{m}\left(F_x - F_{y,f}\sin\delta\right) + r\,v_y, &
\dot{v}_y &= \tfrac{1}{m}\left(F_{y,f}\cos\delta + F_{y,r}\right) - r\,v_x, \\
\dot{r}  &= \tfrac{1}{I_z}\left(l_f F_{y,f}\cos\delta - l_r F_{y,r}\right), &
\dot{\psi} &= r,
\end{align}
with $\dot{x} = v_x\cos\psi - v_y\sin\psi$ and
$\dot{y} = v_x\sin\psi + v_y\cos\psi$.
Lateral tire forces use a linear model with $\tanh$ saturation,
\begin{equation}
F_{y,i} = -F_{y,\max,i}\,\tanh\!\left(\frac{C_{\alpha,i}\,\alpha_i}{F_{y,\max,i}}\right),
\qquad
\alpha_f = \arctan\!\frac{v_y + l_f r}{v_x} - \delta,
\quad
\alpha_r = \arctan\!\frac{v_y - l_r r}{v_x},
\end{equation}
where $F_{y,\max,i} = \mu F_{z,i}$ uses the static axle loads.
The throttle command produces a rear-axle longitudinal tire force capped at
the friction limit, $F_{x,r} = \mathrm{clip}(T\,F_{\mathrm{drive,max}},
\pm\mu F_{z,r})$, and the total longitudinal force includes quadratic drag,
$F_x = F_{x,r} - c_d\,v_x|v_x|$. A friction-ellipse coupling reduces the
rear lateral saturation limit with longitudinal force use:
\begin{equation}
F_{y,\max,r}^{\mathrm{eff}} = F_{y,\max,r}
\sqrt{\max\!\left(1 - \left(\frac{F_{x,r}}{\mu F_{z,r}}\right)^{2},\;
10^{-3}\right)},
\end{equation}
and this effective limit replaces $F_{y,\max,r}$ in the rear tire equation.
The front axle is undriven and uncoupled. Integration is explicit Euler
with $\Delta t = 0.02$\,s.

\section{Environment}
The Gymnasium environment \texttt{DriftEnv} exposes the observation
$[v_x, v_y, r, e_y, e_\psi]$, scaled elementwise by $[20, 10, 2, 4, \pi]$.
The track is a counter-clockwise circle of radius 30\,m centred at the
origin; $e_y$ is the signed radial offset from the centerline and $e_\psi$
the heading error relative to the local tangent. The action is
$[\delta, T]$ with $\delta \in [-0.5, 0.5]$\,rad and $T \in [-1, 1]$.
Episodes start on the centerline with $v_x = 8 \pm 1$\,m/s and run at most
1000 steps (20\,s). They terminate early, with an additional reward of
$-400$, if $|e_y| > 4$\,m or $v_x < 1$\,m/s.

The per-step reward is
\begin{equation}
R = v_x\cos e_\psi + w_1 |\beta| - w_2 e_y^2 - w_3 \dot{\delta}^2,
\qquad \beta = \arctan\frac{v_y}{v_x},
\end{equation}
with $w_1 = 3.0$, $w_2 = 0.5$, $w_3 = 0.002$, and
$\dot{\delta}$ the finite difference of consecutive steering commands.

\section{Training}
The policy is trained with Stable-Baselines3 PPO (\texttt{MlpPolicy},
default two hidden layers of 64 units) for 500\,000 timesteps at a learning
rate of $3\times10^{-4}$ and a discount factor $\gamma = 0.999$; all other
hyperparameters are SB3 defaults. The discount factor was raised from the
default $0.99$ so that the value horizon ($\approx 1/(1-\gamma)$ steps)
covers the full 20\,s episode; with $\gamma = 0.99$ and a $-50$ termination
penalty, training converged to a full-throttle policy that left the track
after 3.7\,s. An
\texttt{EvalCallback} evaluates the policy every 10\,000 steps on 5
deterministic episodes and stores the best model.

\section{Results}
A single deterministic episode of the best saved model was rolled out from
a fixed seed. The episode ran the full 1000 steps (20.0\,s, no early
termination) with a return of 13914, covering 1.5 laps. The mean absolute
slip angle was $6.7^\circ$ ($6.9^\circ$ after the initial transient,
maximum $15.7^\circ$) and the mean absolute lateral error 0.59\,m
(maximum 1.86\,m). Longitudinal velocity settled at 14.4\,m/s on average.
The throttle command varied between 0.26 and 0.82 (mean 0.43); the
steering command had mean $16.8^\circ$ with standard deviation
$14.8^\circ$.
